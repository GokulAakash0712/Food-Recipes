Ven-Pongal

Ingredients

1 cup rice (preferably short-grain rice like Sona Masoori)
1/4 cup split yellow moong dal (lentils)
2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter) or oil
1 teaspoon black pepper
1 teaspoon cumin seeds
1/2 inch piece of ginger, finely chopped
A handful of cashew nuts (optional)
A few curry leaves
Salt to taste
Water
2 tablespoons ghee or oil
1 teaspoon mustard seeds
1 teaspoon cumin seeds
1-2 dry red chilies (broken into pieces)
A pinch of asafoetida (hing)

YIELDS

6 Servings

PREP TIME

45 mins

COOK TIME

1 hr 30 mins

TOTAL TIME

2 hr 15 mins

Directions

  1. Wash Rice and Lentils: Rinse the rice and lentils together in plenty of water until the water runs clear. Drain well.
  2. Pressure Cook: In a pressure cooker, add the washed rice and lentils, along with 4 cups of water. Pressure cook for about 4-5 whistles or until the rice and lentils are soft and well-cooked. If you don't have a pressure cooker, you can cook them in a regular pot, but it will take longer and you'll need to add more water.
  3. Mash: Once the pressure is released from the cooker, open it, and mash the cooked rice and lentils together using a wooden spoon or a potato masher. Add salt and mix well.
  4. Seasoning: In a separate pan, heat 2 tablespoons of ghee or oil. Add black pepper, cumin seeds, chopped ginger, cashew nuts (if using), and curry leaves. Sauté until the cashews turn golden brown.
  5. Tempering: In another small pan, heat 2 tablespoons of ghee or oil for tempering. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter. Then add cumin seeds, broken dry red chilies, and a pinch of asafoetida (hing). Sauté briefly until the spices release their aroma.
  6. Mix: Pour the tempering over the mashed rice and lentils and mix well. Make sure the seasoning is evenly distributed.
  7. Serve: Venn Pongal is ready to be served. It's traditionally served hot with coconut chutney and/or sambar. You can also garnish it with some chopped cilantro.
